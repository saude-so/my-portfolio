Marie Curie

Ms. Marie Curie standing with equipment

  • Full Name: Maria Skłodowska-Curie (born Maria Salomea Skłodowska).
  • Born: November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland (then part of the Russian Empire).
  • Famous For: Pioneer in radioactivity research.
  • Nobel Prizes:
    • 1903 in Physics (shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel) for research on radiation.
    • 1911 in Chemistry for the discovery of radium and polonium.
  • Firsts:
    • First woman to win a Nobel Prize.
    • First person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences.
  • Education: Studied at the University of Paris (Sorbonne).
  • Legacy: Founded the Curie Institutes in Paris and Warsaw, important research centers to this day.
  • Death: Died on July 4, 1934, from aplastic anemia, likely caused by prolonged exposure to radiation.
